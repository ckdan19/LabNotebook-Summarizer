## Hazel Abrahamson

**[Repeat temperature stress prelim — GlycogenGlo results](https://genefish.wordpress.com/2026/06/30/repeat-temperature-stress-prelim-glycogenglo-results/)** · 2026-06-30

Hazel ran a GlycogenGlo assay on oyster samples from a preliminary repeated-temperature-stress experiment (two 48-hour exposures at 32°C vs. 10°C with an 8-day recovery). Key finding: 48h at 32°C significantly depletes glycogen, which fully recovers during rest, but thermal priming in exposure 1 did not reduce glycogen loss in the second heat event. Hazel plans to repeat the experiment at 35°C and will run qPCR on these samples to check whether gene expression tracks the glycogen trends.

**[Starting repeat temp stress at 35°C](https://genefish.wordpress.com/2026/07/06/starting-repeat-temp-stress-at-35-c/)** · 2026-07-06

Following up on the 32°C prelim, Hazel launched a factorial thermal-priming experiment exposing oysters to 35°C for 24 or 48 hours, recovering at 10°C for 7 days, then re-exposing at 35°C. Groups are H/H, H/N, N/H, N/N plus baseline samples collected immediately after exposure 1, with glycogen content and energy-metabolism gene expression as readouts. The schedule runs through July 13, when the final 48-hour second-exposure samples are collected.
