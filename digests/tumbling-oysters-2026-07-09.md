### Gene-Anchored Synteny Between Lean and Siscowet Lake Trout
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 2026-07-02
- **Categories**: Computing, Genomics
- **Key finding**: Haplotigs were purged from both ecotype assemblies (lean reduced from 3.73 Gb to 2.58 Gb, siscowet from 4.0 Gb to 2.84 Gb), genes were re-lifted, and MCScanX synteny maps were built using shared gene IDs as anchors — no aligner needed. The direct lean-vs-siscowet comparison is the richest result: 4,113 collinear blocks and ~78,000 anchor genes, confirming large-scale genome conservation between ecotypes with localized inversions.

### Beyond AUC
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 2026-07-04
- **Categories**: Aquaculture, Computing
- **Key finding**: A meta-analysis of resazurin metabolic assay results shows that dynamic curve-shape metrics — `initial_slope`, `auc_early`, `metabolic_depression_index`, `delta_auc_late_minus_early` — consistently separate oyster families better than endpoint fluorescence alone. The recommended future design is 36°C heat stress sampled at 0, 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 3, and 4 h, with families fully randomized across plates to eliminate plate-confounding artifacts.

### Mapping Structural Differences Between Lean and Siscowet Genomes
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 2026-07-04
- **Categories**: Genomics, Computing
- **Key finding**: The first whole-genome structural variant map between lean and siscowet lake trout was built using RagTag scaffolding, minimap2 alignment, and SyRI variant calling. Translocations (~230 Mb) and highly-diverged regions (~197 Mb) dominate by genomic footprint in the direct ecotype comparison, and ~20% of PAV-flagged regions are independently confirmed by SyRI — producing a high-confidence subset of structural differences for downstream gene annotation work.

### Two-Genome Evidence
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 2026-07-07
- **Categories**: Genomics, Computing
- **Key finding**: Reciprocal PAV (mapping each ecotype's reads onto the other's own genome) identified ~340 ecotype-specific regions per direction; 2,895 genes are now supported by ≥2 independent evidence lines including at least one native-genome source. Critically, re-running gene set enrichment on the native data reveals that the depth-adaptation/calcium-transport signal holds strongly (FDR 9×10⁻⁴), while the previously reported lipid/buoyancy signal does not survive removal of the single-reference bias.

### The case of the missing mussel
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 2026-07-08
- **Categories**: Genomics, Computing
- **Key finding**: Mussel sample 93M — previously labeled a low-quality outlier and excluded — was found to have never been fully processed; only a 10,000-read test run existed. After running the full WGBS pipeline (~41M read pairs), 93M showed completely normal methylation (11.65%, ranked 14th of 24). With all 24 Puget Sound mussels in hand, the baseline methylome shows ~12% CpG methylation in a bimodal on/off pattern concentrated in gene bodies, consistent with the classic invertebrate gene-body methylation signature.

### Reading Methylation on Each Fish's Own Genome
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 2026-07-09
- **Categories**: Genomics, Computing
- **Key finding**: Each ecotype's PacBio reads were aligned to its own genome and methylation was called with `modkit`, then compared gene-by-gene using shared gene IDs to sidestep the coordinate mismatch between assemblies. Of 10,838 genes tested, 63 showed nominal methylation differences but none survived multiple-testing correction, and none of the ~180 previously flagged reference-based DMR genes were confirmed — consistent with the pattern from the two-genome evidence work that single-reference signals weaken on native genomes.
