### The case of the missing mussel
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 07-08-2026
- **Categories**: Genomics, Computing
- **Key finding**: A Puget Sound mussel sample (93M) believed to be a low-quality dropout was found to have been simply never fully processed; after completing the WGBS pipeline it came in 14th out of 24 mussels by overall methylation — perfectly normal. The post also establishes the baseline methylome landscape for all 24 *Mytilus trossulus* samples: methylation is low and sparse (~12%), organized as an on/off mosaic, and concentrated inside gene bodies rather than promoters — the classic invertebrate pattern. A strong negative correlation between CpG depletion and present-day methylation confirms this pattern is consistent with long-term evolutionary history.

### Reading Methylation on Each Fish's Own Genome
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 07-09-2026
- **Categories**: Genomics, Computing
- **Key finding**: To resolve a known reference-genome bias in lake trout methylation analysis, PacBio reads for lean and siscowet ecotypes were realigned to each ecotype's own assembled genome and methylation was compared gene-by-gene rather than position-by-position. Of 10,838 genes tested, 63 showed nominal methylation differences, but none survived multiple-testing correction and none of the ~180 previously flagged reference-based differentially methylated regions were confirmed. The null result is attributed primarily to low statistical power (four fish per group, uneven sequencing coverage) rather than a definitive absence of biology, and the findings consistently support the project's two-genome design as a way to distinguish real ecotype differences from reference artifacts.

### What Genes Reveal About Sea Star Wasting Disease
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 07-10-2026
- **Categories**: Transcriptomics, Genomics
- **Key finding**: Differentially expressed genes from three sea star species (sunflower star, ochre star, leather star) at day 12 of sea star wasting disease exposure were subjected to Gene Ontology enrichment analysis using ortholog-matched gene sets to enable cross-species comparison. Each species showed hundreds of uniquely enriched biological functions, but eight were shared across all three: stress granule assembly, protein kinase signaling, ubiquitin-ligase adaptor activity, trans-Golgi network function, axon cytoplasm, adherens junction organization, and PPAR signaling. This shared core points to a conserved disease response involving emergency stress signaling, protein quality control, and disruption of tissue adhesion — the last being particularly notable given that sea star wasting disease causes tissue disintegration.
