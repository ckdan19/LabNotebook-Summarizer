### Two-Genome Evidence
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 07-07-2026
- **Categories**: Genomics, Computing
- **Key finding**: This post describes a reciprocal presence/absence variation (PAV) analysis of lean and siscowet lake trout, re-running key comparisons on each ecotype's own assembled genome to remove single-reference bias. Using six independent lines of evidence, 2,895 genes were identified as supported by at least two native lines, with 109 genes carrying three or more. Critically, the depth-adaptation calcium signal held up strongly under native two-genome analysis (FDR 9×10⁻⁴), while the previously reported lipid/buoyancy signal did not survive the removal of reference bias.

### The case of the missing mussel
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 07-08-2026
- **Categories**: Genomics, Computing
- **Key finding**: A Puget Sound bay mussel sample (93M) believed to be a low-quality outlier was found to have simply never been fully processed — only a small test run existed, not finished data. After recovering the sample through a full bisulfite sequencing pipeline, 93M ranked dead center among 24 mussels with an overall methylation level of 11.65%. The complete dataset revealed that M. trossulus methylation is low (~12%), organized as an on/off mosaic, and concentrated in gene bodies — the characteristic pattern for shellfish — with CpG depletion serving as a fossil record of long-term methylation history.

### Reading Methylation on Each Fish's Own Genome
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 07-09-2026
- **Categories**: Genomics, Computing
- **Key finding**: To remove single-reference bias from prior methylation comparisons, PacBio reads from lean and siscowet lake trout were each aligned to their own ecotype's assembled genome, and methylation was compared gene-by-gene using shared gene IDs as a common coordinate system. Of 10,838 genes tested, 63 showed nominal methylation differences between ecotypes, but none survived strict statistical correction, and none of the ~180 previously identified reference-based differentially methylated regions were confirmed. The negative result is attributed largely to low power — only four fish per group and uneven sequencing coverage — rather than a definitive conclusion that the earlier signals were artifacts.

### What Genes Reveal About Sea Star Wasting Disease
- **Author**: Steven Roberts
- **Date**: 07-10-2026
- **Categories**: Transcriptomics, Genomics
- **Key finding**: Gene Ontology enrichment analysis was applied to day-12 differentially expressed genes from three sea star species (sunflower star, ochre star, and leather star) exposed to sea star wasting disease, using ortholog-matched genes to enable fair cross-species comparison. Despite each species having hundreds of species-specific enriched functions, eight biological functions were shared across all three — including stress granule assembly, protein kinase signaling, ubiquitin-ligase activity, and adherens junction organization — painting a coherent picture of cells ramping up stress signaling and tissue-integrity disruption. This small shared core, common to three phylogenetically distinct species, is identified as the most likely window into the core mechanism of sea star wasting disease.
